Our History

The Public Library of Las Piñas was established in year 1963 under the administration of Mayor Francisco Antonio. It was first placed on the second level of the old municipal building and had only a few reference books and reference materials. Employees were the most frequent users of the library, but students frequently went there to fill their time with the scant materials provided by Rizal's Provincial Library. Well-intention residents who saw the value of the Public Library gave whatever books they could spare and it wasn't long before others followed in, supplying the library with more reading materials, allowing it to expand its services to an increasing number of library patrons. When Mayor Filemon C. Aguilar took office, he made the municipal Public Library be one of his top priorities, purchasing helpful books, magazines, and foreign reading materials for the growing number of people who read. He also suggested that the Public Library be housed in its own building, which would serve not only for students, but also researchers, office employees, and regular patrons.
